Case Study

UCLA RSVP Platform Redesign

Role Product Designer
Skills UX Research · Wireframing · Mobile-First Design
Tools Figma · Adobe Illustrator · HTML/CSS
Outcome 47% reduction in registration abandonment
Back to Projects

The Challenge

The legacy RSVP system used by UCLA's External Affairs team was outdated, mobile-unfriendly, and confusing for users. The team needed a responsive, streamlined platform that could accommodate various event types and user roles — all without disrupting active event cycles.

My Approach

  • Conducted stakeholder interviews and user surveys to identify pain points
  • Created personas and journey maps based on research findings
  • Designed wireframes and iterated with stakeholder feedback
  • Prototyped and built high-fidelity responsive designs in Figma
  • Delivered assets for development with mobile-first principles

Challenges

Scope creep mid-project required reprioritizing features. I worked closely with developers to phase deliverables and keep the MVP on track without compromising the core user experience.

Key Screens

Outcome

The redesigned RSVP system reduced registration abandonment by 47% and increased mobile usage by 60%. Stakeholders reported measurably improved usability and event coordination efficiency.
